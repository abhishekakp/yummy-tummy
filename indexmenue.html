Tip of the Day

Many people use extra virgin olive oil for high heat sautéing and frying. While in some countries extra virgin olive oil is plentiful and cheap, in most places its much more expensive than pure olive oil. If you buy good quality oils, the only difference between the two is the first and second press. If you use extra virgin olive oil when cooking, the high heat destroys the fruit flavour. Also, if you add it to a to hot pan, the sugars in the fruit can burn and create a bitter taste. Pure olive oil has the same health benefits and is better for higher-heat cooking. Use extra virgin oils to pour over salads or even warm pasta; the full fruit and flavor attributes will be maintained. That’s why you pay more for extra virgin olive oil in the first place.
